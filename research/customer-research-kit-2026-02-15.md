# FarmIQ Customer Research Kit

**Prepared:** 15 February 2026
**Product:** FarmIQ -- SOP-Driven Farm Operations Platform
**Target Market:** Commercial export horticulture farms in Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania, and Uganda
**Research Objective:** Validate product-market fit, understand operational pain points, and quantify willingness to pay among East African export horticulture operations.

---

## Executive Summary

This research kit equips the FarmIQ team to systematically engage, interview, and survey commercial export horticulture farms across East Africa. It contains five interconnected deliverables:

1. **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** -- Defines exactly who FarmIQ serves best, who it serves adequately, and who it should not pursue.
2. **Survey Questionnaire (20 questions)** -- A structured instrument combining screening, pain-point scaling, feature prioritisation, and willingness-to-pay questions, designed for self-completion via Google Forms or KoboToolbox.
3. **Interview Script (30 minutes)** -- A semi-structured guide for one-on-one conversations with farm managers, agronomists, and operations directors.
4. **Outreach Templates** -- Ready-to-send WhatsApp messages, emails, and LinkedIn messages for prospecting, follow-up, and post-engagement thank-you.
5. **Distribution Channel Strategy** -- Specific channels, communities, events, and organisations to reach the target audience at scale.

**Target sample:** 50 survey responses and 15 interviews within 6 weeks.

## Ideal Customer Profile

### Primary ICP (Best-Fit Customer)

| Attribute | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Farm type** | Commercial export horticulture |
| **Crops** | French beans, snow peas, sugar snap peas, herbs (basil, coriander, dill, chives), chillies, baby vegetables, or similar high-value export vegetables |
| **Farm size** | 10--500 hectares under active production |
| **Geography** | Kenya (Naivasha, Nanyuki, Thika, Nairobi corridor, Athi River), Rwanda (Musanze, Bugesera), Tanzania (Arusha, Moshi, Iringa), Uganda (Mukono, Wakiso, Fort Portal) |
| **Export status** | Currently exporting to EU/UK markets; holds GlobalG.A.P., GRASP, or Tesco Nurture certifications (or actively pursuing them) |
| **Labour force** | 50--500+ casual workers per season, managed by supervisors and section managers |
| **Operations management** | Uses paper records, spreadsheets, or fragmented WhatsApp coordination for scheduling, SOPs, and compliance |
| **Decision-maker** | Farm manager, operations director, or owner-operator who controls technology and operations budget |
| **Pain indicators** | Missed spray schedules, inconsistent labour deployment, failed audit findings, harvest forecast inaccuracy, payroll disputes with casuals |
| **Technology readiness** | Smartphone access on-farm (Android dominant), basic internet/mobile data availability, at least one person comfortable with apps |
| **Budget capacity** | Annual technology spend potential of USD 1,200--12,000 (KES 150,000--1,500,000) |

### Secondary ICP (Good-Fit Customer)

| Attribute | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Farm type** | Commercial domestic horticulture with aspirations to export, OR mixed operations (export + domestic) |
| **Crops** | Broader range including avocados, passion fruit, mangoes, macadamia, flowers (where operational SOPs still apply) |
| **Farm size** | 5--10 hectares (smaller but intensive) or 500--2,000 hectares (larger, may need enterprise customisation) |
| **Export status** | Selling to regional markets (Nairobi, Dar es Salaam, Kigali) or beginning export certification process |
| **Labour force** | 20--50 casual workers |
| **Operations management** | Some digital tools in place (e.g., basic ERP, accounting software) but no farm-operations-specific platform |
| **Decision-maker** | Agronomist or farm supervisor who can influence but may not approve budget independently |

### Anti-Persona (Who FarmIQ is NOT For)

| Attribute | Why not |
|---|---|
| **Smallholder subsistence farmers** (<2 ha) | No SOP complexity, no export compliance needs, limited budget, different value proposition required |
| **Large-scale cereal/grain operations** (wheat, maize, rice) | Mechanised operations with different workflow; SOPs less granular; existing precision-ag tools serve them |
| **Flower farms with established ERP** | Already invested in SAP, Sage, or bespoke systems; switching cost too high; FarmIQ overlaps rather than adds |
| **Livestock-only operations** | Fundamentally different operational model; FarmIQ's crop-phase and nutrition SOPs do not translate |
| **Farms with no mobile connectivity** | Cannot use a cloud/mobile platform; infrastructure prerequisite not met |
| **NGO/donor-funded agricultural projects** | Procurement cycles are long, requirements are reporting-heavy, and retention is low once funding ends |

### Qualification Criteria Checklist

Use this checklist during outreach to quickly qualify or disqualify a prospect. A prospect should meet at least 6 of 8 criteria to be considered a primary ICP.

- [ ] Farm grows at least one of: French beans, snow peas, sugar snap peas, herbs, chillies, or similar export vegetables
- [ ] Farm is 10 hectares or larger under active production
- [ ] Farm is located in Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania, or Uganda
- [ ] Farm currently exports or is actively preparing for export (certification in progress)
- [ ] Farm employs 50 or more casual workers during peak season
- [ ] Farm currently manages operations with paper, spreadsheets, or WhatsApp (no dedicated farm-ops platform)
- [ ] Decision-maker (farm manager, operations director, or owner) is reachable and willing to evaluate new tools
- [ ] Farm has basic smartphone and mobile-data access on site

## Interview Script

**Format:** 30-minute structured interview (phone, video call, or in-person)
**Interviewer preparation:** Review the respondent's survey answers (if available) before the call. Have FarmIQ screenshots or a short demo video ready for the Solution Evaluation section.

---

### Part 1: Warm-Up and Context Setting (3 minutes)

**Opening:**

> "Hello [Name], thank you for making time to speak with me today. My name is [Interviewer], and I am part of the FarmIQ team. We are building a farm operations platform specifically for commercial horticulture farms in East Africa, and we are talking to experienced farm managers and agronomists like yourself to make sure we are solving real problems.
>
> This conversation will take about 30 minutes. There are no right or wrong answers -- I am genuinely interested in how things work on your farm today, what frustrates you, and what would make your life easier. Everything you share will be kept confidential.
>
> Before we start, do you have any questions for me?"

**Rapport-building prompts (choose one based on context):**

- "How has the current season been going for you?"
- "I saw that your farm grows [crop]. How long have you been in that crop?"
- "How did you get into horticulture farming?"

---

### Part 2: Current State Exploration (10 minutes)

**2.1 Farm overview**

> "To make sure I understand your operation, could you give me a quick overview of your farm?"

**Probing questions:**
- "How many hectares do you currently have under production?"
- "What crops are you growing this season, and roughly how many blocks or phases do you have planted?"
- "How many casual workers do you employ during peak season? And in the off-peak?"
- "Who are your main buyers, and what certifications do they require?"

**2.2 Operations management**

> "Walk me through a typical Monday morning on your farm. How does the week's work get planned and communicated to the team?"

**Probing questions:**
- "Who decides what tasks happen in which block this week?"
- "How do spray schedules get created and communicated to the spray team?"
- "When a task does not get done as planned -- say a spray is missed or labour is short -- how do you find out, and what happens next?"
- "How do you currently track what has actually been completed versus what was planned?"

**Follow-up prompts:**
- "You mentioned [specific tool/process]. How well does that work for you? What do you wish it did better?"
- "How long have you been doing it this way? Have you tried other approaches?"
- "If I were a new farm manager joining your team, what would be the hardest part of the job to learn?"

**2.3 Team structure**

> "Tell me about the management structure between you and the casual workers in the field."

**Probing questions:**
- "How many supervisors or section managers do you have?"
- "Do your supervisors use smartphones? Do they have access to WhatsApp or other apps during the workday?"
- "How tech-savvy would you say your average supervisor is, on a scale of 1 to 10?"

---

### Part 3: Pain Points Deep Dive (8 minutes)

> "Now I would like to understand the things that keep you up at night or cause the most frustration in running your farm operations."

**3.1 Operational pain**

- "What is the most common reason a task gets missed or done incorrectly on your farm?"
- "When was the last time something went seriously wrong operationally? What happened, and what was the impact?"
- "How often do you discover that a block was sprayed with the wrong product, at the wrong rate, or at the wrong time?"

**Probing prompts:**
- "What did that cost you, roughly -- in terms of money, time, or relationships with buyers?"
- "How did you fix it? What systems did you put in place afterward?"

**3.2 Compliance pain**

- "Tell me about your last GlobalG.A.P. or GRASP audit. How did the preparation go?"
- "Where are the biggest gaps in your records when audit time comes around?"
- "Have you ever had a buyer reject or downgrade a shipment because of documentation issues?"

**Probing prompts:**
- "How many person-hours does audit preparation take each time?"
- "If you could have one report generated automatically for auditors, what would it be?"

**3.3 Labour management pain**

- "How do you handle casual worker attendance today -- is there a register, biometrics, or something else?"
- "How often do payroll disputes arise, and what usually causes them?"
- "How do you decide how many casuals to call in each day?"

**Probing prompts:**
- "What happens when you call in too many or too few?"
- "How much time does your HR or admin team spend on casual payroll each week?"

**3.4 Harvest forecasting pain**

- "How do you currently forecast your harvest volumes for the coming weeks?"
- "How accurate are those forecasts, typically? What happens when you are off?"
- "Have you ever had to buy-in from other farms to meet a buyer commitment, or rejected orders you could have fulfilled with better planning?"

---

### Part 4: Solution Evaluation (5 minutes)

> "Thank you -- this is incredibly helpful. I would like to show you what we are building and get your honest reaction. This is still a work in progress, so critical feedback is just as valuable as positive feedback."

**[Show FarmIQ screenshots, demo video, or describe core features]**

**Core concept description (if no visuals available):**

> "FarmIQ is a platform that lets you set up your farm blocks and crop SOPs once -- both labour tasks and nutrition programmes. Each week, it automatically generates a Gantt-style schedule showing exactly which tasks need to happen in which blocks, how many casual workers are needed, and what products to apply. As tasks are completed, supervisors mark them off on their phones, and you get a compliance dashboard showing what was done versus what was planned. It also forecasts your harvest 8 weeks out based on crop age and historical data, and handles casual worker attendance and payroll."

**Reaction questions:**
- "What is your initial reaction to this?"
- "Which part of what I just described would be most useful to you right now?"
- "What is missing? What would you need it to do that I did not mention?"
- "What concerns would you have about adopting something like this?"

**Probing prompts:**
- "You said [feature] would be most useful -- why is that? What is happening today that makes it so important?"
- "You mentioned a concern about [X]. Can you tell me more about that?"
- "Who else on your farm would need to use this? How do you think they would react?"
- "Would your supervisors be able to use a phone-based app in the field, or would that be a barrier?"

---

### Part 5: Wrap-Up (4 minutes)

**5.1 Ideal solution**

> "If you could wave a magic wand and have the perfect farm operations tool built just for your farm, what would it do?"

- "What would you want to see on your dashboard first thing every morning?"
- "What is the one report or alert that would save you the most time or money?"

**5.2 Budget range**

> "I know budget conversations can be tricky at this stage, but it helps us make sure we build something accessible."

- "Roughly, what does your farm currently spend per month on operational tools -- spreadsheets, software subscriptions, paper systems, the time people spend on coordination?"
- "If a tool like FarmIQ could save you [reference a specific pain point they mentioned], what would that be worth to you per month?"
- "Is there a price range where you would say 'that is clearly worth it' versus 'that is too expensive for what it does'?"

**5.3 Referrals and next steps**

> "Thank you so much for your time -- this has been extremely valuable."

- "Are there other farm managers in your network who you think would be interested in this kind of tool? I would be happy to reach out to them."
- "Would you be interested in being one of our early pilot users when we launch? Pilot users will get [incentive -- e.g., 3 months free, dedicated support, input into feature roadmap]."
- "What is the best way to keep in touch with you -- WhatsApp, email, or phone?"

**Closing:**

> "I really appreciate your time and insights, [Name]. I will follow up with a summary of what we discussed, and I will keep you updated on our progress. Have a great rest of your day."
